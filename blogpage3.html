Rolling to Lisbon

Breaking hearts friendships and travel PRs

Creating 12 day journey plan is not an easy task. Get all the recipes and invoices, find place to stay, find connections, find activities, choose people and mind everyones personal needs while planning. I juggled with most of the tasks, it won't be the most comfortable but as I say travelling shouldn't be anywhere near the comfortable. This is the positive thing about being in charge. You choose the pace, then you just need to push others to march as fast as you.
It's 28 July 5 o ' clock in the morning and we are meeting at train station in Bratislava. Todays goal is to reach Lyon at a reasonable time. The journey will be 14 hours and 4 train changes. In the first train we found a way to cut that time for about 2 hours and pay 40€ less by changing trains. Between the blocks of sleep, thinking about things we forgot and Rick and Morty marathon, the anxious gremlin in my mind was checking platforms from where the trains leave, delays and picturing all the things that could go wrong. The engineering went well, after 12 hours we get to Lyon, buy groceries for breakfast and chill. Wait! No! No chill! We are in fucking Lyon! I am trying to find open café or restaurant otherwise we will die from hunger and my soul will die from lack of cultural experience. With my ear-breaking French I am trying to ask local where we can eat just to get to know that every on level restaurant is closed and only thing left for us are fastfoods. After little circle in the city we end up in Indian - French something selling pullet, rice and sodas. Still to this day I believe it was a sin to buy dinner there (my heart still breaks thinking of that one full bar selling only beverages) but it was cheap, the chicken was tasty and the portion was grand. Then we open a beer and wine and watching a movie we slowly turn to sleep mood.
10 minutes before I go to sleep I see my now former best friend going for a cigarette. I went out to balcony to talk to him. There were a few problems before the journey, I had nasty argument with his girlfriend who was travelling with us and fucked up one friendship with his good good friend. While going to sit on the balcony I ask him: "Got a minute?" He puts out half smoked cigarette and turns away saing he is going to sleep. Ouch, I remember sitting there for 10 minutes, trying to find solid ground after this nonchalant fuck you. From this day, there were only problems with this sweet couple and for others, tactical moves of choosing sides.

Me and Michal, we woke up earlier than others, with a quest of buying fresh bread, croissants and pan au chocolat. We came back to feed our comrades, Filip made best scrambled eggs I have ever had and let's go for a walk through the city. When I say walk, I mean waaalk. Most of the people don't walk that many kilometers in a day when they are touring through mountains. I made the plan and I took the consequences of the never ending threat of bitching about stairs...
Our first stop was Botanical garden, botanical gardens are one of the greatest things in France, they are big you can go inside the greenhouses and they are free! Zumba classes, people around you having pikniquiks wonderful lively France. After a circle around the lake and listening to the open air piano, we stroll further. The croissants which I took as backup plan are already eaten and time of the lunch is coming. Our next stop is vieux Lyon. I don't know where everywhere we went, from bookstores to wonderful sightseeing places but somehow we ended up in a cocktail bar. Me, trying my half broken french again to order us cocktails, instead of lunch we should be having, get us round of very interesting drinks and on the top level of the Lyon we plan our next steps. Trabouletes, Notre Damme... wait isn't Notre Damme in Paris? Dissapointed gremlin in my head slowly understood that every church in France is called Notre Damme.
We start walking, slowly very slowly after refreshing mango-orange-passionfruit potion legs feel little lighter but bitching about the stairs is neverending story. First traboulette and then we get to artisty street with a lot of restaurants. Exploring and translating menu posters in front of the restaurants made hunger in me only grow and with it grows my understanding of apetite and Frenchies. There are 3 course menus, my eyes are blinking and I am trying to drag the whole group to the every restaurant translating the names of the meals a bit more atractive than they really are. Somehow they managed to cut me off. We visit second hand where I buy my favourite pair of jeans and continue to Notre Damme. It's on the hill, but no one is bitching now I don't know why but when the matter is chuch with fancy name everybody's legs turn to little motors and they go and go. I think there was mass at the time but the place looked nice, good view and all that touristy bullshit. Right on the other side was restaurant but prices way overbudget, we uderstood that we are trying to save a little and place for us is not on top of the hill.
Grand probleme where to eat. NOW everyone's hungry and we need to get food soon as possible. I make the racional decision, totally not affected by french menus and split the group. It's only me and Filip left others went for a burger, fries or something like that, basically crime against the god. In our camp the economy starts, we are trying to choose, we're walking down the city centre comparing the meals and prices. There were like 20 restaurants and every one had 20 different meals. I let Filip choose. Honestly I didn't like it, plastic red chairs, it looked more like toy store mixed with goofy 2am bar, but whatever, at least there were locals sitting in this one, the menu is in french and they have Lyon's specialite.
We choosed different menu strategies. Filip ordered things he heard about, he played it safely. On the other hand, I didn't even know what I was ordering I choose the most atractive sounding items from the menu and prepared for an adventure.

Entree

The first course came, Filips tasty and great smelling onion soup. He gave me a piece to taste, I swear, it was like a piece of heaven. Me, it wasn't so good looking it looked like pieces of ham with mayonnaise and salad, after a few bites I realized there was hair in the plate. I tried to put it out but after waitress stopped me tryin, in sign language, to tell me that it is pigs nose and hair is totally normal, I finished the entree, with Filip laughting at me what I ordered.

Plat principal

Filip plyed it safe and ordered steak and potatoes, me, I ordered Lyons speciealite, tripe saussage with tasty sauce and two potatoes. This time when we understood what I have on my plate we bursted out with laughter. Saucisson de Lyon, I want to eat it again and next time with glass of fine wine.

dessert

Now I had enough of surprises so I played it safe with Filip and we ordered creme brulle.
Although description of my food can sound a little bit disgusting, I enjoyed every piece of it. This was something I was longing for and this rollercoster of tastes made me genuinely content. The three course dinner take it's time. Others were already done waiting at the edge of the Rhone. Buy a bottle of wine, sit on the stone border of the flume and enjoy the sunset, that is the most romantic thing you can do in Lyon, at least before coming back to hotel. Pink skies, Notre Damme, vieux Lyon in the background and warm wind, relaxing until there was more lights from the lamps than sky itself. We decided to walk back to the apartment to get a feel of Lyon for the last time. Tomorrow morning we are departing to Madrid and when we come back we will fall to sleep in an instant.

Catch it (not)

Pack early, even earlier is departure of our train. In order to get some fresh air, I decided to take a walk to train station. In maps I found a small bakery and enjoyed last planned French croissant of the journey. I get to the train car with other pirates of this ship and we started planning what to do next, because we do not have seat reservations for the train in Spain. We can not buy them online nor in France. Total madness. On the way, we met pair of other travelers with the same problem, they believe that they will buy tickets on board. We convert to this alternative religion and pray to spanish train god to let us reach Madrid today. Train stops in the station and we are running from door to door being pushed by conductors to find leader of the train and ask him. Spanish train god heard our prayer but only halfway, we buy train reservations for 30€ each although only to Barcelona, but we will be in Spain and buy next tickets there. One person in our group missed the stairs that day, so they had to start bitching to the conductor why we can't go all the way to the Madrid. The conuctor is my spiritual animal from that day, not regarding his hairy spanish chest, he withstand half an hour of bombardation by sentences which contained at least two why's, kindegraten stuff. I would kick the person, no the whole group out of the train but he just ignored it, gracefully. Maybe that is what is the hairy chest about.
After persuading others to not travel without tickets, we get off in Barcelona. Straight to the Renfe clerks to buy tickets. Okay first we stood in queue for half an hour and then they told us that the next train we can buy tickets for is in 5 hours. Fuck me in the ass. We did not have any other option. Morale and mood after this change in plans was really bad. We did not want to go anywhere so we just stayed at brunch place where we ordered only thing on the menu using two spanish words and professional sign language, then visited supermarket to get something to eat because this day will be looooong.
After 5 hour power play we got back to train. In Madrid we've got first metro to our apartment, put our stuff down and run to the restaurant because it was already after 10pm, idk about siesta and Spain but everything was closing at midnight. Only place opened in center was a ancient pub in which Napoleon had his dinner 200 years ago, I swear there was his portrait. Since then they served fruit di mere here. Curiosity led me to try mussles and bite of carrot cake which Filip offered me afterwards. It wasn't a lot but it was the first time I had fruit di mer = unforgetable moment.
After some crusing around the empty night Madrid we get back to our place. I stole the blanket from Michals body and sleep in warm cozy ball.

Now we are only half a day from Lisbon. Refill the supplies and lets roll the train wheels. Portugal has totally different politics in trains, they have no website, so the tickets must be bought in the kiosk and we are hoping the Barcelona incident won't repeat. I got instructions that it is cheaper and more reliable to get flixbus instead of train but we have trains for free so it's the only option and gamble our chances to save 10 euros sounds like a good deal. Our prayers came true and we get on time on every train. Change breaks were used for understanding how to get a train ticket or getting a kebab. It probably wasn't the best deal, the empty place probably meaned something, there was probably too much yogurt or something in it for the 35 degrees that was outside, but getting kebab on Spanish-Portugese borders, Filip and me, we take the risk. The thing that persuaded us to buy the meal was look of the guy in the shop, while trying to pantomime what we wanted, we saw the best turkish moustache ever, it should be called big hipsters erection. Someday I need to come back and ask for the recipe how to get moustache of the turkish kebab god. Spain-Portugal train was horrible. Stacked head on head, motor instead of electricity, no climatization, we were dying, as if it is wasn't enough I needed to shit. Toilet was 10cm from the doors, enjoy the darkness and smell. It was even hotter on the toilet than in whole train. I came back sweating and praying for the normal train. Badajoz-Entroncamento train, we felt like illegal imigrants, but now we were only an hour from the Lisbon. Summer sea breeze, end of the railroad in Sete Rios and now we can try to understand how to use metro and bus here. Easy peasy, on the way to the apartment we got some food, I check out the roof and romanticize about drinking red wine during the sunset there and enjoy sights on our new little neighbourhood.
In the evening we were meant to meet a girl, my potentional future wife.
We've been texting about a month, since erasmus in Lecce. I wasn't really into it, but hoped that her presence will prove me wrong. Most importantly, we needed guide in the Lisbon. She was flirting with me from the second we've met, at least I think. I quickly introduced her to my friends and in return they took care of her for the whole night. We got tour of the night city ate in the finest seamonster restaurant and then half of the group decided to back up to apartment. I stayed in the city with Emma and MFW, she took us to the most romantic views in the city, quite a misunderstanding, then to repay her, we took her home. I was thinking if a fistbumping her on the metro will make her understand my lack of interest, but she was a warrior. Emma, who saw the love somwhere, nailed it with: You two are so cute, that I will puke. The only thing I was horny for at that moment was tomorrow surfing lesson, party and pastel de nata. Fall asleep fast, tomorrow is going to be day of our lives.

The D day

Wake up, eat something, don't forget the swimwear and lets have time of our lives
Meetpoint for the bus was in front of something that I believe looks like palace in Red Square in Moscow. We met there MFW, entered the overcrowded bus and now only half an hour until our feet will fell the sand and our hands will be occupied by surfboard. One necesarry stop in shop and now lets seek out the surf school. Fortunately MFW speaks portuguese, otherwise we would need a translator until the trainer came. Getting into neopren was a battle, putting it on three times, because every time you put it on it was the wrong side. Scratches from the sand, broken nails, but we are only inches apart from our first surfing lesson.

Lesson no.1

You need to try bolas de berlim.

Lesson no.2

The warm up and practise on the ground is crucial.

Lesson no.3

In the water you know nothing.
Processing everything was the hard part. Without initial push from our trainer we wouldnt get far, we tried to keep emotonal highground, I told myself that catching 1 wave out of 5 is good billance.
Our hour was over ): I could spend whole day there but we had a lot and a lot of plans. I understood that surfing is similliar to boudlering, you fall most of the time. I believe that was my strong suit while learning. Do it over and over again, although neopren scratched your kneepits to meat, you can't see through the salt filled eyes and your toes hurt from falling you have only thing in mind: I WILL CATCH ANOTHER ONE.
Now lets step back from the ocean and eat something. Bitoque? Steak with egg? Strange but ok. With all the sea in my mouth everything tasted good, after that THE dessert, bola de berlim were sold out but two sad pastel de nadas waiting for me? They had no chance. We didn't want to go back to Airbnb yet. First plan was to walk through the beach but one of ours stupid heads though lets throw our bodies against the biggest wawes that will come our way. Me as a good male friend joined automatically.
When we were ice cold, thirsty and exhausted like Italian before siesta we took a bus back to our place. Bus changed platforms, no problem, with ola and obrigado you solve out everything. In bus we learned how to sleep standing. Wet swimwear and hair full of salt we came back just to shower it down and prepare for a Lisbon party.
Understanding the dynamics of Lisbon nightlife was quite a challenge, drug dealers on every corner, a litre drinks for 6€. Dont get yourself drunk baby or the urge to take the drugs out of the dealrs casches will take you to grave. It was fun, walking from bar to bar, free entrance shots and looking at Filip in his party mood is always a pleasure. As it goes with sibling fights, every fun ends with tears, here it came to breaking hearts, lying that the flirting was just friendly act would make me puke when it came out of my mouth, if I was sober. A few angry looks from friends of my PFW and our ways parted. Lets flush this night with one guiness and take a taxi back.
I woke up thinking I am dying, throat burns, head hurts and nose is flooded. Quite a regularity for me, but this was extreme. I don't know how, but guys persuaded me with the idea, going surfing again. It was not hard, it was more like: Want to go surfing? Yes. End of persuadisation. We live only once and I can live with a cold, no problem.
Okay, we get off the same place as yesterday, and try to find different surf rental shop. Cheaper prices than yesterday, bit further away from the sea, but still manageable. We are fighting, different boards, from the prior day, but we catched a few. In the end its all about the fun. I don't remeber if we ate anything for the whole day. I fell asleep on the bus, about that I am 100% sure. At the arrival station I bought some sweet pastries and stayed at the airbnb, exhaustion from these two days caught up with me and I had to guard the room while others went to the city.
This was our time in Lisbon, in meantime I got a message that the girl is breaking up with me, but I can keep the blanket she left us yesterday. She didn't deserve the tears, she really did not. I am an asshole for that, when thinking about it now, my romantic life is one big tragicomedy, but there with half of the journey ahead was no time to look back, we had too empty interiary and tomorrow we have to pray that there will be free seats for our journey.
To end this chapter on high note let's talk surfing. I love it, I can't do it but love it. You paddle through the waves, get slammed again and again, but all that exhaustion is worth it, even if you can't catch one, you are connected with the sea. That is the most wonderful thing about it the connection. I have a dream, I have a dream that someday I will go for a surf trip. Somewhere where water is not as cold and surfing all day is the only plan.

Sunday prayings

Okay, don't leave anything at the apartement and cross fingers, because we need to reach Porto today! I take photo of each ID, still didn't sell them on the darkweb, and walk up to the counter. I am sweating, pushing to the ground as strong as I can, just to pray to train gods to let us enter this one. Tudududum, here are your seats for the train that leaves in 5 hours. Everything bad turns to good, so did this little incovenience. While walking to the hill to found meaning of life and seat reservations, we met a small quiet place or it met us, Coffee Volta, bags on the sidewalk everyone 30€ bill for 5 coffes and 2 brunch menus, we were official hipsters of the Lisbon. The people who run the place were Russians, so kind and everything there was spectacular, after about two hours chatting with them and understanding their story I said spasiba took my bag and went looking for another place where we could kill some time.

Right next to bossa

Out of blue, after 20 steps down 3 churches and 5 nonnas we see museum of Fado. I have no idea what it is, but I need to go there, for the sake of putting my backpack down and maybe experiencing the music coming from inside. They do not have place for all our bagages so we split. I don't really care who is going where, because my backpack is the biggest so that makes my priority visit the fado museum twice as big. We split, half of the group stays in fado museum and half finds war museum of some kind. Fado guitar, fado singing, fado music, national treasure. I would want to heart it live because this is only glorification of saudade and other portuguese liabilities.
We ate, we went to the museum so it's not even bad that we had to stay here for another few hours. It only gets us to time problem as the Porto plans are vanishing. We will arive late and exhausted, but I don't care, I slept for 12 hours so I started to search through websites. What can we do in Porto? Pizza place and then jazz? Plan was clear,the only hard thing was finding the Airbnb, I swear platform 3n3/4, up the hill down the hill, gasping for breath, we found it. After that, only rewards, eat pizza, then listen to some music and have a drink which even changed Michal's mind and made him think Jazz is enjoyable and listenable. Before, while waiting for pizzas, argument almost exploded in our group which was already two teams, luckily first plate landed on table and there was no more time left for arguing. In the second part of the concert, on stage came music school students, it made my eyes sweat as it reminded me of my old times...
It's morning, one of last, I found small coffe shop nearby so I start the day with coffe and a book on our little terrace, prearing to explore the city. Emma knows the place, so she will be our guide today. Walk up, down, to the statues then to the majestic McDonalds, but the best place was park and bridge with view and smell from all over the Porto. Walking down the small streets, not even knowing where we are going, thats the real beauty of travelling. Michal was meant to go bouldering with me, but he got aftersurf sickness, probably something from me. Nevermind, let's wait for bus that didn't come and then walk back. I make his/our bed, put him to sleep and then silently go climb. Climbing gym in garage, warm as ass and a few new people, one guy made my eyes shine, he had motivation like me year before, we talk about climbing and then I bump my head against imaginary wall all night as I missed the opportunity to talk to a girl there, but my stupid mind aahhh. Anyways, Porto was the gem of our journey, if I knew, we would stay there the whole 12 days we had for traveling. It is not over yet, I will come back, maybe to believe that I will meet the girl again or to experience the saudade of the Porto bay.

Back ZSSR way, bye

From this day we are not really staying anywhere, it's only fast way back. The plan is that in Barcelona we will sleep on the station, but to Barcelona it is still a long way and as the Iberian Peninsula seat lottery works, we pray again. This time it worked, although we had to buy them for different trains. I had quite good time changing the trains, baguette, coffee and booking Airbnb in Barcelona. On the other side there were Emma and Filip they had about 15 minutes for changing the trains and getting to the other side of the city. They made it, when they texted me that I felt so much relief. Filip was splitting up with us and staying in Barcelona, but Emma was waiting for us with owner gift beers. As we get to the Airbnb quicker than our last 2 cotravellers, we finally take the king bed, yes the bitching when they came was really necessary, but whatever we don't care we are going to the city with the beers in our pockets.
It's middle of the week and we are too exhausted to go party. We want to do something, we need to, the trip is ending. Only thing our minds agree on is walk through night Barcelona, turn back at triumpal arch and end our day with laughts as the bitching now, after a few beers is quite funny.

Paris, city of love

Now it feels we are only rushing. Travelling whole day to Paris, quick photo in front of champ elysees, quick croissant, quick groceries, quick baguettes the travelling ends with night train to Vienna. Luckily we have enough food to stay alive for two days and our exhaustion makes us sleep the whole journey in tetris coupee.

Cherry on top

In Vienna our group is definitely tearing up. I am planning to stay in Vienna for the night, so does Patrik and Izabel. We have the Airbnb but it's opening at god knows when, so I start day with coffe and shopping in Vienna. Then, when I finally have the opportunity to lay my backpack at the Airbnb, I turn back in the door as the room is messy, bedsheets not changed blah. Ok I am going home tonight but before, let's use the last money.
Some bouldering at blockfabrik and then dinner with Emir, guy I met at erasmus in Italy. He brough my mood so high up, feeling of that one friendship is ending and another is being built. Shame that we really didn't stay in contact, we'll see when our paths cross again. We've been to Turkish restaurant for Kebab, to that day I though the kebab is only meaat in Tortilla or bun but this was whole another level, best meal of the trip. After all those seafood dishes, I loved them, but having something so spicy and tasteful was what I needed in that moment so bad. Finish it with baklava and our ways part away.
Back in Bratislava, how it is used to for Slovak rail, the train is late for two hours, I meet some friends, they laught my ass of, that they are taking blabla car, but I have time. Nowhere to rush, I walk across the Trnava to accept my fate. That in a few days I am travelling again.

Chocolate on the cherry

I would want to thank you all, but foremost the people who contributed to this journey the most, to Filip because this has been till now our last big adventure and he has been always my sparring partner in not so convinient ideas. To Emma for not killing me after I said that I am a group leader. To Michal for pretty nights when we shared the bed and he told me fairitales about his love life. To Emir for the best meal of the journey and to others for showing that despite all the problems, travelling is still worth the conflicts.
Everyone bring their own problems to the journey, expectations and underwear. It's hard to blend it all together.
It isn't pretty, neither it is fine all the time. In between of all that sweat, there are wonderful fireworks of emotion.
Only thing we need is the right people or just understanding imperfections of eachother and spoonfull of care for eachother.
Understand the place, don't rush through it. Stay and enjoy the moment. This is something we've had the least time for but that only amplified the delicacy of those moments when we could breath out, sit and enjoy sunset or smalltalk with stranger.
I wouldn't say travelling is pretty or any of the catchy social media bullshit.
It is awfull, it is hard, but it has strange magic in it. Magic that makes you feel more of a human.
But, if anyone ever asks me: how it was?
I'll just say it is a wonderful memory.

Love you all, hope we'll have or had more time for eachother.
